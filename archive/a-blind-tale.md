I have started reading a book "All the light we cannot see" a war novel by Antony Doerr. The book introduces a character Marie who is suffering from rapid vision degeneration and eventually goes blind. I want to take an attempt at writing a short scene in the daily life of Marie. This is me sticking to my writing habit, taking inspiration from wherever i can, i had a range of emotions while i was reading, hence back to the keyboard. So here it goes.

- wakes up to the scent and sounds
- remembers the last time i had seen light when i woke up
- how the clear picture faded into hazy one
- all my senses gripping
- going to the bathroom is dreading experience
- every step reminds me pain, not from the fall i had that is far less painful
- but from the light i cannot see
- my legs have memorized lengths of passage between my bed and the window, depths of each stair, earlier ...
- describe a transition between -> how long has it been and how she coped up with it and how she is now
- end with the frustration of being forced into acceptance
